Recommendation IV-14 (ATCM IV - Santiago, 1966)

SPECIALLY PROTECTED AREAS: LYNCH ISLAND

The Representatives, 

considering that Lynch Island supports one of the most extensive and dense areas of grass (Deschampsia antarctica) known in the Treaty Area and that it provides an outstanding example of a rare natural ecological system, 

recommend to their Governments that the following be inserted in Annex B, Specially Protected Areas, of the Agreed Measures for the Conservation of Antarctic Fauna and Flora:

SPECIALLY PROTECTED AREA No 14

Lynch Island, South Orkney Islands

Lat 60°40'S, long 45°38'W

DESCRIPTION:  A small island, measuring about 500 metres by 300 metres, in Marshall Bay, off the south coast of Coronation Island, South Orkney Islands. The area is shown on the attached map.
